All Technology Is Assistive

by Sara Hendren

The author proposed that all technologies are assistive to every one. Undoing the distinctions between design for disability and design in general yields a couple of benefits. Previously I was thinking that design for disability is an essential part of the overall design process. It helps to allow more people to access the object. However, the author re-evaluate the design approach by looking from the opposite side. Whereas design for disability tackles problems unique to a group of people, it brings new attention to profound knowledge. I see the potential of being inspired by design that ask questions beyond just solving problems.

I also notice the importance of cross-disciplinary research solving new problems with existing knowledge. It is interesting to see scientists, biologists and designers are breaking boundaries between each other and discovering how objects can be used.
