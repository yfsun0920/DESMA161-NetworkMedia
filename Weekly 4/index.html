Empty Models, Flattened Language

by Nora N. Khan

The author proposes a blank xyz model and examines how people interact in the social space. This model inspires me to reflect on my past experiences with the internet and identity in cyberspace.

"But the interface demands you speak about yourself, position yourself, and this particular kind of narcissism and self-involvement that makes you wan to disappear or claim yourself.‚Äù

This quote reminds me of the time when I first started browsing the internet and creating accounts. The platforms usually required me to input a nickname, profile picture, and even a bio. I often struggled to fill out this information, especially the bio. It forced me to consider who I was and how I wanted to present myself on the platform and within the community. As a result, I created many different versions of myself depending on what I wanted to achieve with the account.

I also feel that nowadays people are trying to build more complex models by linking various aspects of their lives, such as connecting personal websites to social media accounts. It's something like constructing a system by creating nodes and employing logic to connect them. It offers more angles or perspectives for others to look at the avatar and imagine its inner life.

I an imagining a future with more diverse ways to represent oneself in virtual spaces. However, it is important to always consider what might be lost with these models.
